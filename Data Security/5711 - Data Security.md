## Academic content

__Purpose:__
This module aims to foster a comprehensive understanding of advanced concepts in security. It will enable students to apply commonly used tools, techniques, and strategies to ensure the protection of data privacy.

__Learning outcomes:__
Upon completing the course, students should be able to: 

 - 	Evaluate core security principles and apply commonly used tools and techniques to protect data privacy.
 - 	Apply cryptographic methods, authentication mechanisms, and access control models in practical contexts.
 - 	Analyse security risks across network layers and evaluate protocols such as IPSec and SSL/TLS.
 - 	Design secure systems that balance technical, organizational, and privacy requirements.
 - 	Interpret policies, laws, and ethical considerations relevant to data security.

__Content:__
 - 	Security concepts: confidentiality, integrity, authenticity
 - 	Cryptography: 
	 - 	symmetric cryptography
	 - 	asymmetric cryptography
	 - 	opportunities and challenges of quantum cryptography
 - 	Key exchange
 - 	Authentication, Digital signature and PKI
 - 	Access control
 - 	Differential privacy
 - 	5-layer TCP/IP protocol model
 - 	IPSec and SSL or similar protocols
 - 	Risk management and secure network design
 - 	Policy, laws and ethics

__Learning and teaching approaches:__
Lectures, exercises, assignments, and self-study.
