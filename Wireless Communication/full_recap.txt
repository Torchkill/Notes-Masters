Amplitude and Phase from I/Q
Any complex baseband sample can be written as
s = I + jQ = Aejθ
▶ Amplitude (radius):
A = |s| = pI 2 + Q2
▶ Phase (angle):
θ = arg(s) = atan2(Q, I )
PSK: constant amplitude, varying phase. QAM: varying amplitude and phase.

Symbol Duration
A symbol is transmitted over a finite time interval.
Key definitions
▶ Symbol duration: Ts (seconds per symbol)
▶ Symbol rate: Rs = 1
Ts
(symbols per second)
Relation to bit rate
▶ Each symbol carries log2 M bits
▶ Bit rate:
Rb = Rs log2 M
Why symbol duration matters
▶ Shorter symbols ⇒ higher data rate
▶ Shorter symbols require more bandwidth
▶ Noise and channel distortion affect shorter symbols more strongly

Resource Allocation in Wireless Networks
Definition: Resource allocation refers to the efficient distribution of available
spectrum, time, and power among multiple users in a wireless network to
maximize performance and minimize interference.
Key Challenges:
▶ Limited Spectrum: Wireless spectrum is a finite resource, requiring
efficient allocation.
▶ Interference Management: Multiple users accessing the network
simultaneously can cause interference.
▶ Quality of Service (QoS): Ensuring reliability, low latency, and fairness
across users.
▶ Scalability: Networks must support an increasing number of users and
devices (e.g., IoT, 5G).
